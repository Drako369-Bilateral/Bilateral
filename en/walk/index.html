Every Tuesday · 7:00pm · Naviglio Martesana

Forty-five minutes to feel which side you start from.

A free group walk along the Martesana. It’s not a class and it’s not a workout: it’s the simplest way to notice how you move — and start choosing it.

What it is

You already walk every day. You’ve never felt it like this.

Walking is the most common gesture there is, and that’s exactly why you do it without thinking: always the same step, always the same side in charge. This walk slows everything down enough for you to notice. You don’t learn a new technique: you learn to feel the one you already use — and, for the first time, to change it.

It’s the simplest way into the Bilateral Method. What becomes fine work in sport, on stage or in rehabilitation, here you touch with the gesture everyone already knows: putting one foot in front of the other.

The forty-five minutes

Three movements, under an hour, and you go home different.

The shape of the walk is always this. The heart — the central part — changes every time, depending on who’s there and what they bring: this is where my work with the person comes in, not a one-size-fits-all drill.

01

Remove

A few minutes standing still, to release the tension the body drags along for no reason. A handful of release exercises that come from Taiji — from a school I’ve practised for more than ten years — but here you don’t learn Taiji: those gestures have been reread for one purpose only, to free the movement and give you back the choice of side.

02

Walk on the gesture

We walk slowly, and observe: which leg is in charge, where the weight never truly passes. Then we try to reverse it — start the step from the side that usually stays silent. It’s clumsy, and that’s right. This is the part I adapt to you.

03

Integrate

The last minutes, still again, with attention on the whole body. It’s the part you can redo on your own, five minutes a day, and it’s what makes what you felt stay.

No preparation needed, no fitness needed, no prior knowledge needed. You need a comfortable pair of shoes and the willingness to walk slowly for once.

One step deeper

If the walk opens something, there’s the session.

The walk is the taste, and it’s free on purpose. But the real work, the one on your gesture — the shoulder that pulls, the hip that always loads, the strike that only comes from one side — happens in a dedicated session: ninety minutes, one to one, on the same three-part principle, but tailored to you. Forty-five euros.

It’s not the step for everyone, and it’s right to meet first on a walk. When you feel you want to go further, you tell me.

Who walks with you

I come from philosophy and a long practice of the body.

My name is Jacopo Fedeli. I’m not a personal trainer and I won’t make you sweat. I come from philosophy, from more than ten years inside an internal Taiji practice, and from training in working with the person. That long practice is where the calm of the gesture comes from; the training is where the way of adapting the central part of the walk to whoever’s in front of me comes from, instead of repeating the same drill for everyone. One last thing

Your body is already doing it.

You always start from the same side, for years, and you didn’t choose it. The question is whether you want to start deciding it. The first step is a walk, and it’s Tuesday.
