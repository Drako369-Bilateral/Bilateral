The tension

Some asymmetries are held in place, not missing strength

Habitual dominance and chronic co-contraction can maintain an asymmetry that pure strengthening only reinforces — the body compensates around the imbalance instead of resolving it. Without distinguishing what needs building from what needs releasing, effort can pull in the wrong direction.

The approach

Two mechanisms, two entry points

Bilateral separates the additive path — building competence through training — from the subtractive path — releasing latent symmetry by reducing co-contraction — and clarifies where release work complements strengthening.

The framing draws on motor-learning research, including the external-focus-of-attention literature, and on neurophenomenology, applied with deliberate caution. It is designed to sit alongside professional clinical care, not to replace it.
