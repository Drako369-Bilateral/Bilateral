The tension

A dominant side is a pattern your opponent can read

Lead always from the same side and you become predictable: your strong line is obvious, your weak line invites attack, and half your options stay closed. A fighter who can genuinely work both sides is harder to read — and can reach lines a one-sided fighter can't.

The approach

Make your lateralization elective

Bilateral combines subtractive release with the science of postural gating — the way limb configuration and stance modulate corticospinal excitability and reflex recruitment.

The goal isn't to erase your dominant side but to make it a choice: you decide which side leads, rather than defaulting to it under pressure. Framed by Sainburg's dynamic-dominance research and the postural-gating literature.
